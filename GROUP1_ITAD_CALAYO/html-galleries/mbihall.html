Mangroves, Beaches and Intertidal Zone

 ›

Gallery IX takes center stage by shedding light on the various coastal ecosystems found in the Philippines. Through this exhibition, visitors are introduced to a wide range of environments, including beach forests, intertidal zones, mud flats, sandy beaches, seagrass beds, and mangrove forests. The gallery aims to showcase the rich biodiversity that thrives within these coastal habitats, both in the marine and terrestrial realms. By featuring a diverse array of organisms commonly found in these ecosystems, visitors gain a deeper understanding of the intricate web of life that exists along the coastlines of the Philippines.


One of the focal points of this exhibition is the floral diversity present in the intertidal zones, with particular emphasis on seagrass beds and various species of mangroves. These vital habitats are brought to life within the gallery, showcasing the unique characteristics and importance of these coastal ecosystems. Furthermore, the exhibition serves as a platform for promoting conservation campaigns that emphasize the significance of protecting and preserving these fragile environments. By highlighting the conservation efforts aimed at safeguarding the intertidal zones, the gallery aims to raise awareness and inspire visitors to become active participants in the preservation of these critical coastal ecosystems.


Gallery IX serves as a captivating window into the coastal ecosystems of the Philippines, allowing visitors to marvel at the beauty and complexity of these natural environments. Through a combination of informative displays, stunning visuals, and engaging exhibits, the gallery provides an immersive experience that educates and inspires visitors about the value of coastal biodiversity and the need for its conservation. By fostering a sense of appreciation and understanding, the exhibition encourages individuals to become ambassadors for the protection of coastal ecosystems and the myriad of organisms that call them home.
