National Planetarium

Cultural Astronomy

Visitors are immersed in the cultural significance of celestial objects. This exhibition explores how different indigenous communities in the Philippines have woven astronomical observations into their beliefs, rituals, and daily lives. From the identification of constellations to the use of celestial events for agricultural practices, visitors gain insight into the deep connection between the heavens and Filipino culture.

Origins and the Evolution of the Universe in Culture and Science

This exhibition presents a captivating blend of mythology, cosmology, and scientific discoveries, inviting visitors to contemplate the profound questions of our cosmic existence. Visitors can explore the creation stories and mythologies from different indigenous cultures in the Philippines, which offer unique perspectives on the origins of the universe and humanity.

READ MORE

Philippine Night Sky

This exhibition is a breathtaking showcase of the country's unique celestial wonders. Visitors can gaze upon stunning visual displays of constellations, planets, and celestial events that grace the Philippine skies. This exhibition not only highlights the natural beauty of the night sky but also encourages visitors to appreciate the importance of preserving dark skies and reducing light pollution.

The Farming and Hunting Section

This exhibition highlight how ancient Filipinos utilized celestial cues to determine the best times for planting, harvesting, and hunting.

The Sea Navigation Section

Visitors can explore how seafaring communities used celestial navigation techniques to traverse the vast Philippine archipelago, ensuring successful voyages and trade connections.

READ MORE

Time Reckoning

Takes visitors on a journey through traditional timekeeping methods and their relationship to astronomical phenomena. From the ancient use of sundials and star clocks to the alignment of structures with celestial events, this exhibition reveals how the movements of the sun, moon, and stars have guided the passage of time and influenced the development of calendars and cultural practices in the Philippines.
